# Loot Goblins Gameplay Writeup (2026-02-23)

## One-Sentence Pitch
Loot Goblins is a chaotic multiplayer roguelite where players scramble through a messy shared dungeon, racing to clear paths, beat bosses, and extract loot before someone else does.

## Game Concept
Loot Goblins is built around one idea: dungeon crawling as a rat race.
- The dungeon is cluttered, dangerous, and partially blocked.
- Everyone is chasing the same opportunities at the same time.
- Fast decisions matter as much as strong gear.

The intended feeling is pressure, greed, and improvisation. Players are never just solving a map; they are competing inside a living scramble.

## The Rat Race: Core Experience Pillar
The rat-race identity is created by combining:
- Shared world progression: one player's actions open or change routes for others.
- Scarcity pressure: valuable rooms, bosses, and timing windows are contested.
- Push-your-luck extraction: go deeper for bigger rewards, or cash out before losing tempo.
- Social chaos: cooperate when useful, compete when profitable.

This turns each run into a messy tactical sprint instead of a clean, single-player route plan.

## Core Gameplay Loop
1. Spawn into the shared dungeon.
2. Explore nearby rooms and discover opportunities.
3. Clear rubble jobs to open new paths.
4. Loot chests and engage bosses for better rewards.
5. Decide when to extract at the entrance stairs.
6. Re-enter with new knowledge and different priorities.

## Dungeon Structure and Exploration
- The dungeon is room-based and discovered progressively.
- Walls can be solid, blocked by rubble, open, or locked.
- Clearing blocked routes permanently expands traversal options.
- Deeper routes generally increase risk and reward.

The map intentionally feels untidy and improvised rather than perfectly authored. That friction is part of the fantasy.

## Jobs (Rubble Clearing)
Rubble jobs create both cooperation and competition:
- Joining a job starts active commitment.
- More helpers speed progress.
- Finishing jobs unlocks routes and accelerates the whole room's tempo.

Player tension:
- Help others now for faster map access.
- Or split off and race to loot while others do the work.

## Chests and Loot Pressure
Chests provide immediate dopamine and route incentives:
- Quick to evaluate, fast to resolve.
- High value when found early in a route.
- Often become mini race objectives when multiple players converge.

Loot is designed to reinforce risky movement decisions: players are rewarded for tempo and route reading, not just combat skill.

## Boss Encounters
Bosses are depth-gated escalation points:
- Higher danger than routine room interactions.
- Better rewards for successful participation.
- Natural gathering points where temporary alliances can form.

Boss rooms amplify the rat-race dynamic: players must decide whether to commit, opportunistically join late, or bypass to secure alternative gains.

## Extraction and Risk Management
Extraction is the strategic climax:
- Returning to the entrance converts progress into secured outcome.
- Delaying extraction can produce larger rewards.
- Staying too long risks lost momentum and missed opportunities.

This decision point is where the game's messy greed fantasy is most visible.

## Intended Player Behaviors
- Rapid route adaptation when dungeon state changes.
- Opportunistic cooperation with strangers.
- Tactical selfishness when time/loot pressure spikes.
- Continuous evaluation of risk vs extraction timing.

## Moment-to-Moment Feel Targets
- Fast interactions with low downtime.
- Constant nearby objectives.
- Readable chaos: disorder that is exciting, not confusing.
- Frequent meaningful choices rather than long passive waiting.

## Multiplayer Identity
Loot Goblins is not a background asynchronous system. It is a live shared race where player presence matters:
- Seeing other players in-room changes choices immediately.
- Completed jobs and defeated bosses reshape the run for everyone.
- Social context creates stories (helpful strangers, loot snipes, last-second extraction pivots).

## What Makes It Different
- "Messy dungeon" as a feature, not a flaw.
- Rat-race pacing instead of methodical crawl pacing.
- On-chain authoritative multiplayer state supporting trust in contested outcomes.
- A design that rewards both execution and opportunism.

## Design North Star
Every feature should answer this question:
"Does this increase meaningful pressure and player-driven chaos without making the game unreadable?"

If yes, it supports the Loot Goblins fantasy.
